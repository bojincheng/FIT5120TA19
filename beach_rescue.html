Preventing Drowning at the Beach 🏖️

Giving immigrant families the tools and knowledge to keep children safe from drowning on Australian beaches

👨‍👩‍👧 Beach Safety Starts with You

🌏 Understand the Risk

Australian beaches have unique dangers you may not have seen before.

  • ⚠️ Waves here are often stronger than other countries
  • ⚠️ Dangerous currents can be invisible
  • ⚠️ Children need closer watching than at home

�� Supervision Saves Lives

Your watchful eye is your child's best protection.

  • ⚠️ Stay within arm's reach of young children
  • ⚠️ Don't rely only on lifeguards - they can't watch everyone
  • ⚠️ Take turns watching with other adults if tired

📚 Learn and Share the Rules

Understanding beach flags can save your family's life.

  • ⚠️ Red & Yellow flags = Only safe swimming area
  • ⚠️ Teach children "No flags = No swimming"
  • ⚠️ Show children how to spot the lifeguard tower

🌡️ Heat & Marine Hazards

Tiredness and panic increase drowning risk.

  • ⚠️ Rest often in shade to prevent exhaustion
  • ⚠️ Stay calm if you see marine life - most won't hurt you
  • ⚠️ Leave the water if feeling tired or cold

🌊 UNDERSTANDING RIP CURRENTS

🌊 Rip Current Simulation

👁️ Visual Signs

Learn to identify these key indicators of rip currents:

  • ⚠️ Deeper, darker water
  • ⚠️ Fewer breaking waves
  • ⚠️ Sandy colored water extending beyond the surf

❌ Danger Zones

Stay away from these high-risk areas:

  • ⚠️ Areas near rocks and piers
  • ⚠️ Deep channels between sand banks
  • ⚠️ Where waves aren't breaking

🏊 If Caught in a Rip

Follow these steps to stay safe:

  • 1️⃣ Stay calm - rips won't pull you under
  • 2️⃣ Float and raise your hand for help
  • 3️⃣ Swim parallel to the beach to escape

🚫 Never...

Avoid these dangerous actions:

  • ⚠️ Try to swim against the rip
  • ⚠️ Panic or exhaust yourself
  • ⚠️ Swim at unpatrolled beaches

🌊 ESCAPING RIP CURRENTS

1

Stay Calm

Rip currents won't pull you under - they only pull you away from shore.

  • 🧘 Control your breathing
  • 💪 Conserve your energy
  • 🌊 Float with the current
2

Signal for Help

Make yourself visible to lifeguards and other beachgoers.

  • 🙋 Raise one arm high
  • 👋 Wave for attention
  • 🗣️ Yell for help if close to others
3

Swim Parallel

Escape the rip by swimming parallel to the beach.

  • ↔️ Swim along the beach
  • 🏊 Use the waves to help you
  • 🎯 Aim for breaking waves

🆘 BEACH RESCUE GUIDE

🛟 Try the Rescue Simulation

1️⃣ Recognize Emergency

Know the signs of a swimmer in distress.

  • 👀 Person showing distress signals
  • 👋 Waving arms or calling for help
  • 💦 Head bobbing in water

2️⃣ Alert Help

Quick action can save lives.

  • 🏃 Find nearest lifeguard
  • 📞 Call 000 immediately
  • 📍 Note exact location

3️⃣ Safe Rescue

Help safely without endangering yourself.

  • 🔍 Look for flotation devices
  • 🪢 Throw rope or buoy if available
  • ⚠️ Don't enter water unless trained

🗣️ Communication

  • 📢 Yell clear instructions to victim
  • 🌐 Ask for interpreter if needed
  • 📱 Stay on phone with emergency

🏥 After Rescue

  • 👨‍⚕️ Get medical check even if seems fine
  • 🔄 Learn from the experience
  • 📝 Report incident to lifeguards
